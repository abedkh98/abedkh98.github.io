How MINT Works

2
EMG sensors map each muscle to a perpendicular cursor direction — co-activation appears as diagonal movement on screen.
3
Patients play a retro video game at home — hitting off-diagonal targets trains muscles to decouple through operant conditioning.

Key Engineering Challenge

The stimulation artifact corrupts the EMG signal needed to close the loop — a classic chicken-and-egg problem. The solution: a TSLM trained to decode intent from artifact-corrupted windows, treating the artifact as structured noise rather than trying to remove it.

The broader vision: a soft wearable patch worn during morning therapy, triggered by movement intent decoded from the skin surface — delivering personalized, continuous spinal neuromodulation at home.

Wagon Condition Monitoring — real-time fault
detection for passenger trains.
◈ Completed · Iran Railway

A multi-sensor condition monitoring system for passenger rail wagons, measuring six subsystems in real time — wheel flat detection, axle bearing temperature, brake health, transformer performance, battery/charger status, and refrigerator temperature. Alerts are displayed onboard for the technical officer, logged to a database, and transmitted via SMS/internet to maintenance teams before the train arrives at the station.

System Architecture
Sensor Layer
ADXL345 3-axis MEMS accelerometer (wheel flat detection) · MLX90614 IR temperature sensor (axle bearing, brake disc, transformer) · ACS712 Hall-effect current sensor · CT current transformer · pressure sensor HOTJ0010FLCK · DS18B20 digital temperature (refrigerator).
I/O Boards (ATmega MCUs)
Distributed ATmega328P / ATmega2560 boards handle per-subsystem signal acquisition. Communicate via I2C, SPI, and 1-Wire. Slave boards aggregate to Master via serial; Master connects to central PC via Ethernet (TCP/IP).
Signal Processing (LabVIEW)
Central LabVIEW application receives all sensor streams, applies synchronization across boards with different sampling rates, runs fault detection algorithms, and logs data to a SQL database with timestamps.
Alarm & Reporting
Relative thresholding compares each wheel/disc against the fleet mean — eliminates false alarms from speed, load, and ambient temperature. Faults trigger onboard display alerts, SMS, and web-service notifications to the maintenance team.

Key Algorithm — Wheel Flat Detection

Wheel flats generate repetitive high-amplitude impulses in the axle accelerometer signal at a frequency proportional to train speed. Two parallel algorithms detect these:

Autocorrelation
Identifies repeating patterns regardless of waveform shape. Speed estimate from GPS + accelerometer data fusion (weighted inverse-variance). More robust to noise; higher memory use.
Virtual Mass-Spring
Simulates a damped resonator in software. Three parallel resonators (center ± offset) track wheel rotation frequency. Resonance amplitude ∝ flat severity. Low memory, real-time capable.
// Axle accelerometer · periodic impulse from wheel flat · resonator response
Relative Threshold Alarm Logic

Absolute thresholds produce false alarms because temperature, speed, and braking all affect readings. The solution: compare each sensor against the mean of all 8 axles on the same wagon. External factors affect all wheels equally — only a defective component deviates.

Wheel flat: flat severity vs. mean of 8 wheels · relative threshold · configurable duration
Axle bearing / brake disc: IR temperature vs. mean of 8 sensors · eliminates ambient + speed effects
Brake pipe pressure: low-pass filter (τ ≈ minutes) detects chronic low pressure · ignores normal braking transients
Transformer: imbalance, overload, short circuit, efficiency drop, overheating — 6 independent fault types
