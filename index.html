MINT — Myoelectric Interface
for Neurorehabilitation
● RCT Complete · 2026

After stroke, muscles co-activate abnormally — for example biceps and deltoid fire together, preventing straight reaching. MINT trains stroke survivors to break these patterns at home through a gamified EMG interface, unsupervised, for 6 weeks.

How MINT Works
1
After stroke, muscles co-activate abnormally — for example biceps & deltoid fire together, preventing straight reaching.
2
EMG sensors map each muscle to a perpendicular cursor direction — co-activation appears as diagonal movement on screen.
3
Patients play a retro video game at home — hitting off-diagonal targets trains muscles to decouple through operant conditioning, for 6 weeks.
4
The 3-muscle group showed strongest results: 7.8× better arm recovery vs. sham across the 59-patient RCT.

Neural Decoding · LFP · Real-Time BMI · Motor Cortex
Brain–Robotic Arm BMI — decoding movement force
from the motor cortex.
⬡ Published · IEEE TNSRE

A real-time brain–machine interface that decodes motor cortex local field potential (LFP) signals to continuously control a robotic arm in freely behaving rats. The system can switch in real time between sensor control (force sensor) and brain control (LFP-decoded force), enabling direct comparison of decoded versus actual force during behavioral experiments.

System Architecture
Behavioral Task + Neural Recording
Rats trained to press a force sensor to receive a water reward. 16-channel microwire arrays implanted in the forelimb area of M1 record LFPs simultaneously during all trials.
LFP Feature Extraction
High-gamma LFP features extracted from 16 channels in real time. Adaptive artifact rejection removes motion and cable artifacts without contaminating the neural signal band.
Force Decoding
Continuous force decoded from LFP population activity. Decoded force drives the mechanical arm in brain control mode — matching the pattern of actual lever-press force.
Real-Time Switching
Experimenter can switch between sensor control and brain control mid-session. Live GUI shows decoded force, real force, raw 16-channel LFPs, and animal behavior simultaneously.

Embedded Systems · Signal Processing · LabVIEW · IoT
Wagon Condition Monitoring — real-time fault
detection for passenger trains.
◈ Completed · Iran Railway

A multi-sensor condition monitoring system for passenger rail wagons, measuring six subsystems in real time — wheel flat detection, axle bearing temperature, brake health, transformer performance, battery/charger status, and refrigerator temperature. Alerts are displayed onboard for the technical officer, logged to a database, and transmitted via SMS/internet to maintenance teams before the train arrives at the station.

System Architecture
Sensor Layer
ADXL345 3-axis MEMS accelerometer (wheel flat detection) · MLX90614 IR temperature sensor (axle bearing, brake disc, transformer) · ACS712 Hall-effect current sensor · CT current transformer · pressure sensor HOTJ0010FLCK · DS18B20 digital temperature (refrigerator).
I/O Boards (ATmega MCUs)
Distributed ATmega328P / ATmega2560 boards handle per-subsystem signal acquisition. Communicate via I2C, SPI, and 1-Wire. Slave boards aggregate to Master via serial; Master connects to central PC via Ethernet (TCP/IP).
Signal Processing (LabVIEW)
Central LabVIEW application receives all sensor streams, applies synchronization across boards with different sampling rates, runs fault detection algorithms, and logs data to a SQL database with timestamps.
Alarm & Reporting
Relative thresholding compares each wheel/disc against the fleet mean — eliminates false alarms from speed, load, and ambient temperature. Faults trigger onboard display alerts, SMS, and web-service notifications to the maintenance team.

System Overview — Sensor Placement on Wagon
Sensors distributed along the wagon: brake pipe pressure · wheel flat (accelerometer) · axle bearing temperature · battery/charger · transformer voltage/current · internet relay via ethernet hub
LabVIEW Dashboard — Onboard GUI
Main screen: left column shows status LEDs (green=OK, red=fault) for all 8 subsystems. Tabs for axle temperature, wheel flat, brake, transformer, charger. Live gauges for voltage, current, pressure, and temperature.
